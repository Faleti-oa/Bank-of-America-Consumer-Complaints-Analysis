# Bank-of-America-Consumer-Complaints-Analysis
A data analysis project for Bank of America Complaints. The project includes data cleaning, analysis, visualization, and actionable recommendations.”
## Introduction
This project analyzes customer complaints made against Bank of America between 2017 and 2023. The goal is to uncover complaint trends, identify high-risk product areas, evaluate response performance, and recommend actions to improve customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.
## Project Description
The analysis covers the end-to-end data process: loading, cleaning, modeling, visualization, and interpretation. Two Power BI dashboards were created — Overall Complaint Overview and Regional Performance — to display complaint patterns by product, issue, state, and response type.
## Project Aim
To analyze Bank of America’s consumer complaint data to understand where, why, and how complaints occur and to recommend steps that can improve response speed and service quality.
## About the Dataset
• Source: Bank of America Consumer Complaints Dataset (2017–2023)
• Records: 62,516 complaints across 51 states
• Key Columns: Product, Issue, State, Date Received, Timely Response, Company Response, Submission Channel
## Tools Used
• Microsoft Power BI
## Importing the Dataset
The raw dataset was imported from Excel into Power BI. Data was modeled into tables for product, issue, state, and response categories before building the visuals.
## Data Cleaning & Transformation
• Removed duplicate complaints
• Standardized product and issue categories
• Ensured correct data types for dates and text
• Created calculated measures for timely response rate and complaint totals
## Data Modeling
Relationships were built between “Product,” “Issue,” and “State” tables. DAX measures calculated totals and percentages for KPIs (e.g., total complaints, timely response rate, complaint share by region).
## Data Analysis
Key performance metrics analyzed:
• Total Complaints
• Timely Response Rate
• Complaint by Product
• Complaint by Issue
• Complaint by Region and State
## Data Visualization
Dashboard Page 1 – Overall Complaint Overview

Dashboard Page 2 – Regional Performance

## Key Insights
1. Checking & Savings Accounts had 24,814 complaints — the highest among products.
2. “Managing an Account” was the top issue (15,109 cases).
3. The South Region recorded 23,152 complaints, the highest by region.
4. California (13,709), Florida (6,488), and Texas (4,686) had the most complaints by state.
5. 93.77% of complaints were handled on time.
6. Complaint trend peaked in 2022 (12,953 complaints) and dropped in 2023 (9,131).
7. Most responses were “Closed with Explanation” (41,044 cases).
8. Web submission was the dominant channel (45,423 complaints).
9. Fraud/scam and credit card issues remain significant concerns.
10. Total complaints spanned 51 states with varying service patterns.
## Recommendations
• Improve customer support training in the South Region.
• Audit checking and savings account processes to reduce errors.
• Strengthen fraud detection and digital security systems.
• Optimize web complaint portal for faster acknowledgment.
• Automate timely response tracking to reach > 95%.
• Introduce state-specific service enhancement plans.
## Conclusion
The analysis revealed that Bank of America’s complaints are most frequent in the South Region and involve checking and savings accounts. Addressing operational issues, enhancing digital experience, and improving response timeliness can significantly boost customer satisfaction and brand trust.
